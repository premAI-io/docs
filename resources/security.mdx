## How your data is protected

### Encryption

| Data | Protection |
|---|---|
| In transit | TLS (HTTPS) on every connection |
| Workspace files | Encrypted at rest on disk |
| Database | Encrypted at rest |
| OAuth2 tokens | Encrypted in dedicated secrets management |
| Conversations | Encrypted at rest |

### Authentication

- **Clerk** handles user authentication — passwords, multi-factor auth, social login.
- **OAuth2** for every third-party app connection — your passwords are never seen or stored.
- **Per-user isolation** — one user's credentials cannot access another's data.

### Infrastructure

- Dedicated per-user sandbox containers.
- Network policies restrict cross-user communication.
- Credentials delivered via secure boot files, never in environment variables.
- Automatic token refresh before expiry.

## What Fluso accesses

### Only what you connect

Fluso only sees apps you've explicitly connected. If Slack isn't connected, Fluso knows nothing about your Slack activity.

### Permissions you control

For every connected app, you see and approve specific permissions:

| App | Permissions | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Gmail | Read emails, send emails | Summarise the inbox and draft replies. |
| Calendar | Read events, create events | Show your schedule and schedule meetings. |
| Slack | Read messages, send messages | Search conversations and post updates. |
| GitHub | Read repos, write issues/PRs | Work with your code. |

### What Fluso never does

- ❌ Delete your emails or files.
- ❌ Change your account settings.
- ❌ Share your data with third parties.
- ❌ Train AI models on your private data.
- ❌ Access apps you haven't connected.
- ❌ Take write actions without your approval.

## Your knowledge graph

The memory Fluso builds — people, projects, conversations — is:

- Stored in your isolated workspace.
- Inaccessible to other users.
- Deletable (entries or the whole graph).
- Built only from apps you've connected.

<Info>
  The knowledge graph makes Fluso more helpful by remembering context. You're always in control. If something shouldn't be tracked, exclude specific people, projects, or topics with a simple instruction.
</Info>

## For teams

Team plan admins **can**:

- See which apps team members have connected (for management).
- Set security policies.
- Manage user access.

Team plan admins **cannot**:

- Read team members' messages.
- Access team members' knowledge graphs.
- See team members' files without explicit sharing.
