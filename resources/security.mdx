Your data is yours. Fluso is built so it stays that way.

## The principles

You control access. When you connect an app, you see the exact permissions Fluso wants and approve only the ones you're comfortable with. Revoke at any time, from Fluso or from the app's own settings.

Action requires approval. Reading happens freely. Anything that writes to another system — sending email, posting to Slack, opening a PR, creating an event — waits for your explicit approval. You see what Fluso is about to do and decide.

Your workspace is isolated. Files, conversations, tasks, and the knowledge graph live in your own sandbox. Other users never see them.

Anything is deletable. Individual entries, the whole knowledge graph, an entire connected app's data, your account. All of it goes when you say it does.

## Encryption and infrastructure

Connections use TLS. Workspace files are encrypted at rest. The database is encrypted at rest. OAuth tokens live in a dedicated secrets manager, encrypted, never in environment variables. Conversations are encrypted at rest.

Authentication runs through Clerk: passwords, MFA, social login. Third-party app connections use OAuth2, which means your passwords are never seen. Per-user isolation guarantees one user's credentials cannot access another's data.

Each user gets a sandbox container of their own. Network policies prevent cross-user communication. Token refresh happens before expiry, automatically.

## What Fluso accesses

Only what you connect. If Slack isn't connected, Fluso has no idea what you've discussed in Slack.

For each connected app, you see and approve the specific permissions:

| App | Permissions | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Gmail | Read emails, send emails | Inbox triage, drafting replies. |
| Calendar | Read events, create events | See your schedule, schedule meetings. |
| Slack | Read messages, send messages | Catch up, search, post updates. |
| GitHub | Read repos, write issues/PRs | Work with your code. |

What Fluso never does:

- Delete your emails or files.
- Change your account settings.
- Share your data with third parties.
- Train AI models on your private data.
- Access apps you haven't connected.
- Take write actions without your approval.

## Your knowledge graph

The memory Fluso builds — people, projects, decisions, conversations — is stored in your isolated workspace. No other user sees it. It's deletable in pieces or in whole. It only ever builds from apps you've connected.

If something shouldn't be tracked, just say so:

> *"Don't track anything related to Project X."*

> *"Forget everything about this vendor."*

The graph makes Fluso more useful, but you're always in control.

## On Team plans

Admins can:

- See which apps members have connected (for management).
- Set security policies.
- Manage user access.

Admins cannot:

- Read members' messages.
- Access members' knowledge graphs.
- See members' files without explicit sharing.
