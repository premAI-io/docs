A common ask: *"Can you put together a competitive analysis of the top five AI agent platforms? I need it for the board on Thursday."*

It's Monday. You have three days. The old plan involves a lot of tabs.

## The old version

Monday: Google "AI agent platforms", open twenty tabs, start reading, take notes, get distracted, come back, take more notes. Three to four hours.

Tuesday: keep reading. Try to organise the findings. Realise you're missing pricing data. Go searching. Three to four hours.

Wednesday: build the deck. Write slides. Find images. Format. Polish. Five to six hours.

Total: twelve to fourteen hours over three days. Most of which is finding things, not thinking about them.

## With Fluso

Monday, 9:00 AM. You ask:

> *"Research the top five enterprise AI agent platforms. Compare pricing, features, target market, funding, and competitive positioning. Create a 12-slide board presentation with citations."*

Five to ten minutes later, two things land in chat.

A research report: market overview and sizing, profiles of the five platforms with comparison tables, pricing analysis, funding and growth data, a section on competitive advantages and vulnerabilities, and 25-plus cited sources.

A presentation: twelve slides, professionally laid out, charts comparing pricing and features, key takeaways framed for board discussion, speaker notes that contain the supporting context.

Monday, 9:15 AM. You read the deck. Tweak three slides where you have a stronger opinion than the model. Add a couple of insights only you would know.

Monday, 10:00 AM. Done. The deck is ready. Three days early.

About an hour total, including the time you actually spent thinking.

## Variations

For something lighter:

> *"Quick research on Rippling's product, pricing, and recent funding. Five-bullet summary, no deck."*

For when you want sources scoped:

> *"Research enterprise AI adoption trends. Sources from 2025 or later. Prefer industry analyst reports over blogs."*

For combining external research with your own context:

> *"Research our top three competitors. Combine public information with what we've discussed about them in emails and Slack."*

The third one is genuinely different from what you'd get from search alone, because it weaves in the things only your knowledge graph knows.

## Tips that move quality

Specificity drives output quality the most. *"Top five platforms compared on pricing, features, target market, and funding"* beats *"research AI platforms"* by a lot. The model can do almost anything; what you tell it to do is what you get.

Ask for the artifact, not the research. *"Create a 12-slide board presentation with citations"* gives you the deliverable. *"Research it"* gives you a report you'd then have to convert.

Verify the load-bearing claims before you're standing in front of the board. The citations are linked. Click through.
