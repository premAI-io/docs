This is the fastest path to having Fluso doing useful work for you. Five minutes if you don't get distracted.

For the deeper take on permissions and the rest of the catalog, see [Integrations](/integrations/overview). When you're ready to level up after a few days of use, go to [Going deeper](/going-deeper).

## Create the account

Sign up at [app.fluso.ai](https://app.fluso.ai). Google or GitHub is fastest. Email and password works too. Free, no card, full feature access while you decide.

You land in the chat. Sidebar on the right has projects, tasks, and settings.

## Connect Gmail

In **Settings → Connectors**, click **Connect** on Gmail. Google's OAuth screen opens. Sign in with the account you want Fluso to assist with.

Three permissions, all of them necessary for the basic features:

- Read your messages, so Fluso can summarise the inbox, search, and pull tasks out of actionable mail.
- Send mail, so it can draft replies. Drafts always get reviewed by you before they go.
- Read headers, for categorisation.

There's no "delete email" or "change account settings" permission anywhere in the flow. Fluso has no path to either.

## Connect Calendar

Same flow on the Google Calendar card. It needs read access to see your schedule and write access to put events on it. With both connected, the morning brief and meeting prep start working.

Slack and GitHub are good additions, but only when a workflow asks for them. Most of Fluso's value on day one comes from these two alone.

## Send your first prompts

Three minutes, in roughly this order. Try them in real life, not on the docs page.

The simple ones — they should respond immediately and prove the connections work:

> *"Summarise my unread emails from today."*

> *"What's on my calendar tomorrow?"*

A bit more useful:

> *"Create a task: review the Q2 report, due Friday."*

> *"Draft a reply to the last email from Sarah. Tell her I'll have estimates by EOD tomorrow."*

The kind of thing you couldn't easily do before:

> *"Find emails from the last week about the product launch and summarise the decisions."*

> *"Prep me for my 3pm. Who's there, and what have we discussed before?"*

The first day's prompts will feel a little stiff. Fluso doesn't know your projects, your contacts, or your house style yet. By day three it's tighter; by week two it sounds right the first time.

## Make a project (optional)

Projects are folders for context. Each one has its own files, memory, and tasks, so noise from unrelated work doesn't bleed in. You can create one from the sidebar or just ask:

> *"Create a project called Q2 Planning and add my recent emails about Q2 to it."*

Useful naming patterns: a specific client (`Acme Corp`), an internal initiative (`Q3 Launch`), a recurring obligation (`Board Reporting`), or a personal bucket (`Personal`).

## Skills you'll probably use within the first week

Skills are capabilities Fluso reaches for when the request needs them. You don't install them. They activate when you ask for the right thing:

- *"Make a 5-slide deck about our product roadmap."*
- *"Process this meeting transcript and create tasks from the action items."*
- *"Summarise this YouTube video: [link]."*
- *"Research the enterprise AI market and give me a cited report."*
- *"Create a logo for my startup. Modern, minimalist, blue."*

## What to do over the first week

A loose plan that builds the habit without trying to install everything at once.

**Day one.** Run a morning brief: *"Morning brief"* gets you inbox triage, today's schedule, what's due, and any follow-ups. Skim, approve any drafts you like. That's the first habit installed.

**Day two.** Connect Slack if your team uses it. Try a prompt that crosses two integrations: *"What is the team saying about the launch in #product?"*

**Day three.** Process a meeting. Record your next call (Zoom, Meet, Teams will do this), then *"process this meeting transcript"*. Two minutes of review beats an hour of post-meeting cleanup.

**Day four.** Make something from a description. A doc, a deck, a spreadsheet, an image. Pick whatever you start from a blank page most often.

**Day five.** If you write code, connect GitHub. Try *"show me how authentication works in this repo"* and watch Fluso reference your actual files.

By Friday, you should be running a morning brief instead of opening Gmail, processing meetings same-day, and creating drafts from descriptions. If two of those three aren't sticking, the [Workflows](/workflows/morning-brief) section walks each of them through with specific prompts.
