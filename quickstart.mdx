You can be productive with Fluso in about five minutes. This page covers the fastest path. For more detail on permissions and connectors, see [Connect your apps](/connect-apps).

## Step 1 — Create your account

Go to **[app.fluso.ai](https://app.fluso.ai)** and sign up. You can use:

- A Google account (fastest)
- A GitHub account
- Email and password

<Note>
  Fluso is free to start. You get full access to try everything before choosing a plan. No credit card required.
</Note>

Once signed in, you land in the main Fluso interface — a chat window with your projects, tasks, and settings in the sidebar.

## Step 2 — Connect Gmail

Go to **Settings → Connectors** and click **Connect** on the Gmail card.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Authorize with Google">
    Google's standard OAuth flow opens. Sign in with the account you want Fluso to assist with.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the permissions">
    Fluso requests:

    - **Read your emails** — to summarize, search, and extract tasks.
    - **Send emails** — to draft and send replies, always with your approval.
    - **View metadata** — headers used for categorization.

    Fluso never asks for permission to delete email or change account settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You are connected">
    Gmail now shows as **Connected** in your settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 3 — Connect Google Calendar

Click **Connect** on the Google Calendar card and grant access:

- **Read calendar events** — to see your schedule and check availability.
- **Create and modify events** — to schedule meetings on request.

<Tip>
  Gmail and Calendar together unlock most of Fluso's day-to-day value. Slack and GitHub are great to add as you discover workflows that need them — see [Connect your apps](/connect-apps).
</Tip>

## Step 4 — Send your first messages

Try these in order, simple to compound:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start simple" icon="play">
    > *"Summarise my unread emails from today"*

    > *"What's on my calendar tomorrow?"*
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Get more useful" icon="zap">
    > *"Create a task to review the Q2 report — it's due Friday"*

    > *"Draft a reply to the last email from Sarah — say I'll have estimates by EOD tomorrow"*
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="See the real power" icon="sparkles">
    > *"Find emails from the last week about the product launch and summarise the decisions"*

    > *"Prep me for my 3pm meeting — who's attending and what have we discussed before?"*
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Combine multiple apps" icon="layers">
    > *"When's the earliest I can schedule a one-hour meeting with the engineering team this week?"*

    > *"A client emailed about API rate limits — find related Slack discussions and draft a response"*
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Info>
  Fluso gets better as you use it. Your first messages establish context. By day two or three, Fluso knows your projects, your frequent contacts, and your preferences.
</Info>

## Step 5 — Create a project (optional)

Projects keep work organised. Each project has its own files, memory, and context.

Ask Fluso:

> *"Create a project called 'Q2 Planning' and add my recent emails about Q2 to it"*

Or use **Projects** in the sidebar to create one manually.

| Scenario | Project name |
|---|---|
| A specific client or partner | "Acme Corp" |
| An internal initiative | "Q3 Launch" |
| A recurring area of work | "Board Reporting" |
| Personal | "Personal" |

## Step 6 — Browse skills

Skills are specialised capabilities — creating PDFs, generating images, processing meetings, running deep research. They activate automatically when you ask for something they can do.

| Skill | Try saying |
|---|---|
| **PDF / Word** | *"Create a PDF report of my Q1 metrics."* |
| **Presentations** | *"Make a 5-slide deck about our product roadmap."* |
| **Meeting intelligence** | *"Process this meeting transcript and create tasks from the action items."* |
| **Video summaries** | *"Summarise this YouTube video: [link]."* |
| **Deep research** | *"Research the enterprise AI market and give me a cited report."* |
| **Image generation** | *"Create a logo for my startup — modern, minimalist, blue."* |
