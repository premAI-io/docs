Fluso handles the entire meeting lifecycle: **before** (prep), **during** (you focus on the conversation), and **after** (summary, tasks, follow-ups).

## Calendar management

### See your schedule

> *"What's on my calendar today?"*

> *"Show me my schedule for tomorrow."*

> *"What does my week look like?"*

Fluso doesn't just list events — it adds context. For each meeting, it shows who's attending and what it's about based on your past interactions.

### Find available time

> *"When am I free for a 30-minute call tomorrow?"*

> *"Find a 1-hour slot this week when the whole engineering team is available."*

> *"I need 2 hours of focus time today — when can I block it?"*

Fluso checks across your calendars (including shared ones), accounts for tasks and existing events, and suggests times that actually work.

### Schedule meetings

> *"Schedule a 30-minute standup tomorrow at 9am."*

> *"Create a meeting with Sarah next Monday at 2pm about the Q3 review."*

> *"Block 3-5pm today for focused work — no interruptions."*

> *"Set up a recurring 1:1 with my direct reports every other Tuesday."*

## Meeting intelligence

Upload a transcript or recording and Fluso handles everything.

### Supported formats

- **Transcripts:** `.txt`, `.vtt`, `.srt`, `.docx`, `.md`
- **Recordings:** audio files (auto-transcribed)
- **Sources:** Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Otter.ai, Fireflies, Granola, and more

### What you get

> *"Process this meeting transcript."*

| Deliverable | Description |
|---|---|
| **Executive summary** | 3-5 paragraphs of what was discussed and decided. |
| **Decisions** | Bulleted list of every decision made. |
| **Action items** | Tasks with assignees and deadlines (when mentioned). |
| **Follow-up email** | Draft email to attendees summarising the meeting. |
| **Slack update** | Draft message for your team channel. |
| **Knowledge graph** | Attendees and topics linked to memory for future context. |

## Meeting analysis

Fluso can analyse your communication patterns across meetings:

> *"Analyse my meetings from the last 2 weeks."*

| Pattern | What Fluso checks |
|---|---|
| **Speaking balance** | Do you dominate, or stay too quiet? Ratio vs. others. |
| **Clarity** | Are your points landing? Frequent clarifying questions? |
| **Filler words** | Frequency of "um", "like", "basically", "you know". |
| **Interruptions** | Who interrupts whom? |
| **Action orientation** | Do meetings end with clear next steps or vague intent? |
| **Trends over time** | Are patterns improving or regressing? |

<Card title="Sample analysis">
- Speaking ratio: 42% — balanced.
- Filler words: 8/min — above average; try the "pause instead of um" technique.
- You interrupted others 4 times across 6 meetings; you were interrupted 2 times.
- Trend: filler words down from 12/min last month; speaking ratio improving.
- Recommendation: focus on filler words. Otherwise effective.
</Card>

<Info>
  Meeting analysis is private. Only you see your analysis. It's designed to help you improve, not to judge.
</Info>

## Workflows

### The end-to-end meeting

<Steps>
  <Step title="Before">
    *"Prep me for the 2pm client call"* → Fluso delivers attendee profiles, recent context, open items.
  </Step>
  <Step title="During">
    Focus on the conversation. Record if you want a transcript.
  </Step>
  <Step title="After">
    *"Process this meeting"* → Fluso delivers summary, tasks, email draft, Slack update.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow-through">
    Fluso tracks the tasks it created. If Lisa misses her deadline, you'll know.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Meeting-free day setup

> *"Block off Thursday for deep work — decline or reschedule any non-urgent meetings."*

> *"Show me which meetings this week I can safely skip."*

### Recurring meeting audit

> *"Review my recurring meetings — which have low attendance or could be emails?"*

## Tips

<Tip>
  **Record your meetings.** Most video tools support recording. A transcript means Fluso can process the meeting completely — no manual notes.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Prep is the superpower.** A one-minute prep request saves 15 minutes of scrambling and makes you look more prepared.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Process right after.** Upload immediately while everything is fresh. Tasks get created and follow-ups go out same-day.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Always inform participants if you're recording. Follow your organization's policies on meeting recording and transcription.
</Warning>
