Fluso does the digging, reading, and synthesising — so you get a structured answer, not a list of links.

## Deep research

Ask for research and Fluso reads multiple sources, verifies facts across them, and delivers a cited report — not just a summary.

### How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search">
    Fluso queries the web across multiple sources.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read">
    Reads the most relevant articles and pages.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cross-reference">
    Verifies claims across sources.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Synthesise">
    Structures findings into a coherent report.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cite">
    Every claim links back to its source.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### What a deep research report looks like

<Card title="Structure">
**Executive summary** — 2–3 paragraphs of the most important findings.

**Key findings**
- Finding 1 with supporting evidence [source]
- Finding 2 with data points [source]
- Finding 3 with context [source]

**Detailed analysis** — section-by-section breakdown with sources cited inline.

**Competitive landscape** *(if applicable)* — comparison table with key differentiators.

**Recommendations** — what to do with this information.

**Sources** — 15–30 cited sources with links.
</Card>

## Data analysis

### Statistical analysis

Fluso runs proper statistical tests and explains results in plain English:

> *"Our A/B test ran for 2 weeks. Control: 1,000 visitors, 50 conversions. Variant: 1,000 visitors, 65 conversions. Is this significant? What's the effect size? Should we ship it?"*

<Card title="Output">
- **Test:** two-proportion z-test
- **Result:** statistically significant (p = 0.042)
- **Effect size:** Cohen's h = 0.15 (small to medium)
- **95% CI:** [0.3%, 2.7%]
- **Interpretation:** the variant likely improves conversion by 1.5 percentage points (5.0% → 6.5%). Real effect, modest but meaningful.
- **Recommendation:** ship the variant. Improvement is real, risk minimal. Monitor for two more weeks to confirm.
</Card>

### What Fluso can analyse

- A/B test results (conversion, revenue, engagement).
- Survey responses and ratings.
- Before/after comparisons.
- Correlation analysis.
- Sample size calculations for experiment planning.

### Data quality auditing

> *"Audit this dataset before we use it — any quality issues?"*

Fluso checks for:

- **Missing values** — gaps and patterns.
- **Inconsistencies** — conflicting formats, contradictory entries.
- **Outliers** — values that don't make sense.
- **Validity** — wrong types, impossible values.
- **Duplicates** — repeated and near-duplicate entries.

You get a prioritised list ranked by severity and impact.

## Tips

<Tip>
  **Be specific.** "Top 5 enterprise AI platforms, compared on pricing, features, target market, and funding — with citations" gets a much better report than "research AI platforms."
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Ask for the format.** Add "create a presentation" or "make it a PDF" to your research request and Fluso delivers the final product, not just the research.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Citations matter.** Verify critical claims before making consequential decisions.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Research is based on publicly available information at query time. Facts can change. For high-stakes decisions, verify key claims independently.
</Warning>
