Connect Gmail and Fluso becomes your inbox assistant. It reads, organises, drafts, and extracts action items — so you spend minutes on email instead of hours.

## What Fluso can do

- **Triage your inbox** — what needs attention, what's FYI, what's noise.
- **Summarise emails and threads** — the bottom line, instead of making you read everything.
- **Search intelligently** — find what you need with natural language.
- **Draft replies** — responses in your voice, ready for review.
- **Auto-extract tasks** — actionable emails become tracked tasks automatically.
- **Track follow-ups** — know who hasn't responded and when to nudge.

## Inbox triage

### Daily brief

> *"Summarise my unread emails from today."*

A typical response:

| Bucket | What's in it |
|---|---|
| **Needs action (3)** | Sarah Chen: contract review by Friday • CFO: Q2 numbers for board • Engineering: production hotfix approval |
| **FYI (5)** | Weekly metrics • Product roadmap update • Vendor pricing change • 2 newsletter digests |
| **Noise (7)** | Notification digests, marketing emails, automated alerts |

### Focused triage

> *"Which emails need a reply today?"*

> *"Show me only the urgent emails."*

> *"What emails mention the Acme deal?"*

> *"Are there emails from my boss I haven't responded to?"*

## Summaries

### Single email

> *"Summarise the latest email from Legal."*

Fluso extracts: who sent it, what they want, the deadline, and what you need to do.

### Full thread

> *"Summarise the email thread about the vendor negotiation."*

Fluso reads the whole history and gives you who said what, decisions made, where things stand, and what's pending.

### Topic across the inbox

> *"Summarise all emails from last week about the product launch."*

Fluso finds matching messages across your inbox and synthesises them into one summary.

## Smart search

You describe what you want. Fluso constructs the search:

| You ask | Fluso finds |
|---|---|
| *"The email where Thomas sent the pricing spreadsheet"* | Emails from Thomas with spreadsheet attachments. |
| *"Emails from last month about the server migration"* | All matching messages in that timeframe. |
| *"Emails with 'invoice' and 'overdue'"* | Messages matching both terms. |
| *"Unread emails older than 3 days"* | Messages you might have missed. |
| *"Emails with attachments from the design team"* | Sender + attachment filter. |

Fluso also handles complex queries:

> *"Emails from @acme.com in the last 2 weeks that mention 'contract' and have attachments."*

> *"Starred emails from April about the budget."*

## Drafting replies

### Reply to an existing email

> *"Reply to Sarah's email — say I've reviewed the proposal and have three changes. Detailed feedback by Thursday."*

> *"Draft a response to the client — thank them for the feedback, confirm we'll implement the changes by next sprint."*

> *"Reply briefly to the team: sounds good, let's discuss in tomorrow's standup."*

### Adjust tone

> *"Make it more formal."*

> *"Softer — this is a sensitive topic."*

> *"Shorter — just the key points."*

> *"Add more detail about the timeline."*

### Compose new emails

> *"Email the engineering team announcing the code freeze and what it means for deployments."*

> *"Compose an introduction email to the new client we met at the conference."*

<Warning>
  Fluso never sends an email without your explicit approval. Every draft is shown for review before anything goes out.
</Warning>

## Automatic task extraction

Fluso scans your inbox for messages that ask you to do something and creates tasks automatically — no manual entry.

### What it does

<Steps>
  <Step title="Scan">
    Fluso periodically reads your recent unread mail.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Identify">
    It picks out messages where someone is asking you for action.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create">
    A task appears with title, priority, deadline (when mentioned), and a link back to the original email.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Examples

| Email says | Task created |
|---|---|
| "Can you review this by Friday?" | **Review document** — High — Due Friday |
| "Please send me the Q2 numbers" | **Send Q2 numbers to [sender]** — Medium |
| "We need someone to present at the all-hands" | **Prepare all-hands presentation** — Low |
| "URGENT: production issue needs your sign-off" | **Approve production hotfix** — Urgent |

### What doesn't get captured

Fluso skips:

- Newsletters and marketing.
- "Thank you" and acknowledgement messages.
- FYI updates that don't ask for action.
- Calendar invites (handled by the Calendar integration).

### Managing auto-tasks

You can:

- Adjust priority or deadline.
- Mark as not a task if Fluso misidentified.
- Add notes or context.
- Link to related projects.

### Weekly digest

> *"Give me a weekly summary — what happened in my email this week?"*

Fluso surfaces key conversations, decisions made, pending items, and people you haven't responded to.

### Project-specific email

> *"Find all emails related to the Acme project and summarise where things stand."*

Fluso searches across your inbox, groups by thread, and gives you a status update — even if the emails span months and multiple people.

## Tips

<Tip>
  **Connect Gmail first.** Email is where most work originates. Connecting Gmail gives Fluso the richest source of context about your work.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Let auto-tasking run for a few days.** Task extraction gets better as Fluso learns which of your emails typically contain action items.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Drafts are starting points.** Fluso's drafts are excellent, but add your personal touch — especially for sensitive or high-stakes emails.
</Tip>

<Info>
  Fluso uses OAuth2 to access Gmail. Your password is never seen or stored. Revoke access anytime from your Google account or from Fluso's Connectors page.
</Info>
