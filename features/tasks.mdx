Fluso doesn't just manage tasks — it **finds them for you**. Tasks appear automatically from emails, meetings, and Slack. You focus on doing the work; Fluso handles the tracking.

## How tasks get created

### Automatically from emails

Fluso scans your inbox for messages that ask you to do something and creates tasks with the right priority, deadline, and context.

<Card title="Example">
**Email from CFO:** *"Can you have the Q2 numbers ready for the board meeting Friday? Also, Sarah from Legal needs sign-off on the vendor contract by EOD Thursday."*

**Fluso auto-creates:**
1. **Prepare Q2 numbers for board meeting** — High — Due Friday — Source: CFO email (linked)
2. **Sign off on vendor contract** — High — Due Thursday — Source: CFO email (linked)

**You did nothing.** They just appeared in your task list.
</Card>

### Automatically from meetings

When you upload a transcript, Fluso extracts every action item:

| Spoken in the meeting | Task created |
|---|---|
| *"I'll follow up with Legal about the contract."* | Follow up with Legal about contract |
| *"Sarah will own the redesign and have designs by next Friday."* | Complete redesign designs (Sarah, due next Fri) |
| *"We need to decide on the Q3 budget by the 20th."* | Decide Q3 budget (due May 20) |

### From Slack conversations

> *"Find any action items from the #engineering Slack channel this week and create tasks."*

### Manually when you ask

> *"Remind me to submit the expense report by Monday."*

> *"Create a task: Update onboarding docs with new API changes, due next sprint."*

## Organising tasks

### Categories

Tasks are auto-categorised so you can focus on one area at a time:

| Category | Example |
|---|---|
| Emails | Reply to client proposal |
| Work | Complete Q2 report |
| Personal | Book dentist appointment |
| Finance | Submit expense report |
| Travel | Book flights for conference |
| Health | Schedule annual checkup |
| Education | Complete online course module |
| Shopping | Order new laptop |

### Priorities

Every task gets a priority. Fluso auto-detects from the source message:

| Priority | When | Detected from |
|---|---|---|
| **Urgent** | Today | "URGENT", "ASAP", "by EOD" |
| **High** | This week | "by Friday", "this week", from your manager |
| **Medium** | Should do, not critical | General requests without urgency signals |
| **Low** | Nice to have | "when you get a chance", "no rush" |

### Scheduling

Tasks with dates appear next to your calendar events:

> *"Show me my day."*

| Time | What |
|---|---|
| 9:00 — 10:00 | Standup meeting |
| 10:00 — 11:30 | Task: Prepare Q2 report (Friday, High) |
| 11:30 — 12:00 | 1:1 with Lisa |
| 1:00 — 2:00 | Task: Review PR #142 |
| 2:00 — 3:00 | Client call |
| 3:00 — 4:00 | Focus time (blocked) |
| 4:00 — 5:00 | Task: Reply to vendor emails |

Tasks and meetings, one view. No separate task manager needed.

## Managing tasks

### Updating

> *"Mark the vendor contract task as done."*

> *"Make the Q2 report task urgent — board meeting moved up."*

> *"Move the presentation task to next week."*

> *"Assign the design review to Sarah."*

### Bulk actions

> *"Reschedule all overdue tasks to tomorrow."*

> *"Mark all newsletter-related tasks as low priority."*

> *"Archive completed tasks from the Q1 project."*

### History

Every change is tracked:

- Source and creation time.
- Status changes (pending → in progress → completed).
- Priority changes.
- Deadline adjustments.
- Assignment changes.

Useful for weekly reviews (*"What did I complete this week?"*), status updates (*"Status of the Acme deliverables?"*), and remembering context (*"Why did we deprioritise this?"*).

## Tasks across projects

Tasks belong to projects. When you're inside a project, Fluso surfaces relevant tasks:

> *"Show me all tasks for the Q2 Planning project."*

> *"What's still open on the Acme project?"*

> *"Add a task to the website redesign project: update the hero copy."*

## Reminders and deadlines

### Due-date tracking

> *"What's due this week?"*

> *"Show overdue tasks."*

> *"What deadlines are coming up in the next 3 days?"*

### Recurring tasks

> *"Remind me every Monday to review weekly metrics."*

> *"Recurring task: submit expense reports every other Friday."*

### Notifications

When a deadline approaches, Fluso can alert you in-app, by email, or via Slack.

## Tips

<Tip>
  **Let Fluso find your tasks.** The real magic is automatic creation. Connect your email and calendar, process meeting transcripts, and watch your task list build itself.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Review and adjust each morning.** Auto-tasks get priority right ~80% of the time. Two minutes of review handles the rest.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Use projects.** Tasks for "Acme Corp" should live in the Acme project. This keeps your main view clean.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Mark things done.** Completion preserves the task in history — you can always look back at what you did.
</Tip>
