<Update label="2026-07-02" description="Desktop 1.2.0" tags={["Desktop"]}>

**Highlights**
- Edit a delivered file right in the chat, and your manual changes are kept.
- A per-chat Fast mode for the turns where you want a quick answer over a deep one.
- Replies come back faster, and multi-step turns finish sooner.
- The app is time-aware: relative times like "tomorrow at 9" resolve to your local timezone.
- Finished replies settle cleanly, with no stranded answers or a stuck "Working".

**Polish & experience**
- Consistent icons and better dark-mode contrast across the app.
- The green "chat finished" dots in the sidebar survive a reload instead of vanishing.
- Links only trigger on real web addresses, so ordinary dotted text isn't turned into a link.

**Connectors & sync**
- Email stays draft-first: the assistant writes to your Gmail drafts and sends only after you confirm.
- Adding tool servers like Linear from chat now completes sign-in instead of failing partway.
- When you ask about your email, the assistant reads across your recent messages instead of occasionally checking only the latest one.

**Assistant reliability**
- The assistant no longer strands a finished reply, lingers on "Working" after it's done, or replays a previous turn's answer into a new message.
- Brief provider hiccups mid-turn recover on their own instead of ending the reply.

**Smarter assistant**
- The inline editor opens Markdown, documents, and code, with a per-thread list of the files in that chat. Your edits are handed back to the assistant instead of being overwritten the next time it works on the file.
- Fast mode lives in the composer and stays set for that thread, and the composer shows which model you're on.
- The assistant is clearer about what it can and can't do, and points you to the docs for how it works rather than claiming features that aren't set up.

**Plans & limits**
- After you upgrade your plan, the whole app reflects it right away instead of showing the old plan for a few minutes.

**Reliability, privacy & trust**
- Connected-account actions are locked to your own account.
- File uploads work again while confidential mode is on.

</Update>

<Update label="2026-06-19" description="Desktop 1.1.0" tags={["Desktop"]}>

**Highlights**
- Confidential mode on the Max plan: run your chats and voice through end-to-end encrypted inference, so your work stays on a private path.
- Manage your connected tool servers in one place, with per-tool permissions and a clean reconnect.
- The assistant recovers from mid-task hiccups instead of hanging on "Working".
- Report a bug from inside the app and it becomes a tracked issue the team follows up on.

**Polish & experience**
- Attachments stay put when you switch chats: image previews and files you haven't sent yet are kept per conversation.
- Sidebar chat titles start from your first message and refine once your intent is clear, and never overwrite a title you set yourself.
- The bug-report dialog now takes PDF and Markdown files, and you can paste files straight in.

**Connectors & sync**
- See your connected MCP servers on one screen, set what each tool is allowed to do, and reconnect without dropping your current connection. Credentials are stored encrypted on the server, not in local files.
- More tool servers connect on the first try and stay connected, so providers like Supabase and Linear no longer quietly expire.
- If you have many connected apps, the ones past the first ten no longer show as "not connected".

**Assistant reliability**
- The assistant recovers from mid-task network and provider hiccups instead of leaving you on "Working", and stops cleanly when you ask it to.
- Voice notes come back as proper sentences, with punctuation and capitalisation.

**Smarter assistant**
- Emails the assistant drafts arrive as properly formatted HTML and sign off with "Sent via fluso.ai", which you can turn off or rename. You still approve every message before it sends.

**Reliability, privacy & trust**
- On the Max plan, turn on "Confidential APIs" from settings or the chat composer. It's a one-way switch: while it's on, file uploads, image generation, and background syncing pause so nothing leaves the private path.
- A new Beta badge on the home screen sets expectations and gives you a one-click way to report a bug.
- API keys your tools use can no longer be read back through the assistant's shell.

</Update>

<Update label="2026-06-13" description="Desktop 1.0.9" tags={["Desktop"]}>

**Highlights**
- A cleaner, more cohesive app after a top-to-bottom design pass.
- Search your threads right from the sidebar.
- The assistant got noticeably smarter at research, drafting, and visuals.

**Polish & experience**
- Normalised typography, spacing, and layout across the whole app. Sidebars, headers, buttons, cards, and empty states all feel more consistent.
- A clearer step-details panel in the work log.
- On macOS, the window frame now follows your light/dark theme. No more dark strip at the top on launch.

**Find things faster**
- Search past conversations from the sidebar, with matches highlighted.
- Telegram chats are now labelled in the sidebar so you can tell sources apart at a glance.

**Smarter assistant**
- Deep Research asks a clarifying question when your request is ambiguous instead of guessing, and uses refreshed web-search connectors.
- Draft emails and Slack messages get an automatic polish pass before you approve and send, so they read like a person wrote them.
- Slide decks use richer visuals for pitch and brand decks, while data-heavy decks stay clean.
- Sharper image generation, now powered by GPT-5.4 Image 2.

**Reliability, privacy & trust**
- Backend services now run in the EU region for European users.
- Fewer noisy banners: the "Technical Issue" banner only appears when something user-facing is genuinely broken.
- Security hardening across the app from a recent external review.

</Update>

<Update label="2026-06-04" description="Desktop 1.0.8" tags={["Desktop"]}>

**Highlights**
- The app updates itself now. New versions install quietly in the background.
- Drop your connection mid-answer and the assistant picks back up, with nothing lost.
- Syncs recover on their own instead of getting stuck.

**Updates & installation**
- Background auto-updates: once you're on 1.0.8+, new builds install on their own. A sidebar dot shows when one is ready, and Settings has a "Check for updates" button.
- Windows and Linux now use the same clean, frameless window style as macOS.
- Windows builds are properly signed (no more "unknown publisher" warning), and the taskbar shows the Fluso icon.

**Connectors & sync**
- Gmail and Slack syncs recover on their own from rate limits and hiccups. If a Gmail connection truly expires, you're clearly prompted to reconnect.
- Queued syncs honestly show as "queued" instead of pretending to run.
- Google Drive: once a file is uploaded, clicking it again opens it in Drive instead of uploading a duplicate.

**Assistant reliability**
- If your connection drops or your laptop sleeps mid-response, your answer is preserved and finishes on its own.
- In-progress tasks are no longer lost if something restarts behind the scenes.
- The "Worked for X seconds" timer is honest now, and empty voice notes no longer break the conversation.

**Plans & onboarding**
- Free and Sponsored Pro plan limits were raised, with more headroom before you hit a cap.
- The name, role, and preferences you set during onboarding now carry across the whole app.

**Reliability & offline**
- Open the app with no network and you get a clean "No internet connection" screen with a Try Again button that auto-recovers when you reconnect.

</Update>
