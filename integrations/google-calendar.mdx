Calendar is the natural pair to Gmail. Together, they unlock the morning brief, meeting prep, and the full meeting workflow.

## What it unlocks

| Capability | Example |
|---|---|
| **View schedule** | *"What's on my calendar tomorrow?"* |
| **Find free time** | *"When am I free for 30 minutes on Thursday?"* |
| **Schedule meetings** | *"Create a meeting with Sarah Monday at 2pm."* |
| **Meeting prep** | *"Brief me on my 3pm meeting."* |
| **Task sync** | Tasks with dates appear alongside calendar events. |
| **Block focus time** | *"Block off 2-5pm for deep work."* |

For the full feature deep dive, see [Calendar & meetings](/features/calendar).

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings → Connectors">
    Click **Connect** on the Google Calendar card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize with Google">
    Same OAuth flow as Gmail. If you already authorized Gmail with the same account, this is even faster.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review permissions">
    | Permission | Why |
    |---|---|
    | Read events | View your schedule and check availability. |
    | Create and modify events | Schedule meetings on your behalf. |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Calendar shows as **Connected**. Setup time: about 30 seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Disconnecting

From **Settings → Connectors**, click **Disconnect** on the Google Calendar card. All tokens are deleted immediately.

You can also revoke from [Google Account → Security → Third-party apps](https://myaccount.google.com/permissions).

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Fluso doesn't see a calendar I expect">
    Make sure the calendar is shared to the account you authorized. Fluso only sees calendars visible to that Google account.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Times are off by hours">
    Confirm your timezone in Fluso settings matches your Google Calendar timezone. Mismatches can shift event times.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can't create events">
    Verify the **Create and modify events** permission is granted. Reconnect if the prompt asked for read-only access.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
