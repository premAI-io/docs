Fluso works best connected to your tools. Each integration gives Fluso more context about your work and more ability to help you. Connect any app in two clicks — no passwords, no API keys.

## How integrations work

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect">
    Click **Connect** on an app in **Settings → Connectors**. You're redirected to that app's authorization page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize">
    Grant Fluso the specific permissions it needs. You see exactly what it can and can't do.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use">
    Fluso can now use that app as part of your conversations. Just ask for what you want.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Fluso uses OAuth2 for every integration. Your passwords are never seen or stored. You can revoke access at any time from Fluso or from the connected app itself.
</Info>

## Managing integrations

Open **Settings → Connectors** at any time to:

- See what's connected and active.
- Connect new apps.
- Disconnect apps you no longer use.
- Review permissions for each app.
- See when tokens were last refreshed.

## Security and privacy

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How are credentials stored?">
    OAuth2 tokens are encrypted at rest in a dedicated secrets manager. Your passwords are never seen or stored.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can Fluso act without my permission?">
    Never. Any action that modifies external data — sending email, posting to Slack, creating PRs — requires your explicit approval first.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens when I disconnect?">
    All tokens are deleted immediately. Fluso stops accessing that app. You can also revoke access from the connected app itself.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can teammates see my connected apps?">
    No. Your integrations are private to your account. Team plan admins can see which apps members have connected (for management) but cannot access the data.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

For more, see [Security & privacy](/resources/security).

## Tips

<Tip>
  **Start with Gmail and Calendar.** Those two save hours in your first week. Add Slack and GitHub as workflows demand them.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **More integrations = more context.** The more apps you connect, the more Fluso understands about your work. But start with the ones you actually use daily.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Audit monthly.** Disconnect apps you no longer use. A few well-used integrations beat many unused ones.
</Tip>
