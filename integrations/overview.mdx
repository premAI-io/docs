Each app you connect gives Fluso more to work with. More context to understand what you mean. More capability to act on what you ask. The catalog runs to over a hundred services; four of them carry most people's day.

## Working model

You click **Connect** on a card in **Settings → Connectors**. The app's own OAuth screen opens. You see exactly what permissions Fluso wants and approve the ones you're comfortable with. After that, Fluso can use the app inside your conversations. There's no key to copy, no callback URL to set, no shared password anywhere.

Tokens live encrypted in a dedicated secrets store. You can revoke from Fluso or from the source app's settings. Disconnecting deletes the token immediately.

## Managing connections

Open **Settings → Connectors** to see what's connected and active, when each token was last refreshed, what each app has access to, and to disconnect anything you've stopped using. An audit once a month is a good habit. A few well-used connections beats many half-forgotten ones.

## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What does Fluso actually store?">
    OAuth tokens, encrypted, in a dedicated secrets manager. Conversations, files, and the knowledge graph live in your isolated workspace. Passwords are never seen.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can it act without me approving?">
    No. Reads happen freely. Anything that writes to another system — sending mail, posting to Slack, opening a PR, creating an event — waits for your explicit approval.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens when I disconnect?">
    The token is deleted. Fluso loses access immediately. Anything already in your knowledge graph stays unless you delete it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can teammates see my apps on a Team plan?">
    Admins can see which apps a member has connected so they can manage access. They cannot read the data.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
