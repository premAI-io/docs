Connect Slack and Fluso adds team context to everything else it does — meeting prep gets richer, email replies pull in relevant Slack threads, and you can catch up on a channel in seconds.

## What it unlocks

| Capability | Example |
|---|---|
| **Catch up** | *"What did I miss in #engineering today?"* |
| **Search** | *"Find messages about the server migration from last month."* |
| **Post updates** | *"Share the meeting summary in #product"* (with your approval) |
| **Team context** | Meeting prep enriched with relevant Slack discussions |
| **Action item detection** | Tasks created from Slack messages that assign you work |

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings → Connectors">
    Click **Connect** on the Slack card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose workspace">
    Pick the Slack workspace you want to connect. You can connect multiple workspaces if needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize channels">
    Grant Fluso access to public channels, selected private channels, or all channels you have access to. You decide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review permissions">
    | Permission | Why |
    |---|---|
    | Read messages in authorized channels | Catch you up, search history, surface action items. |
    | Send messages | Post updates — always with your approval. |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Slack shows as **Connected**. Setup time: about 30 seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Cross-app workflows

Slack becomes powerful when combined with other integrations:

> *"A client emailed about API rate limits — find related discussions in #engineering and draft a response."*

> *"Process this meeting transcript and post the summary in #product."*

> *"Prep me for the 3pm meeting — pull in any Slack discussions about the topic."*

## Disconnecting

From **Settings → Connectors**, click **Disconnect** on the Slack card. All tokens are deleted immediately.

You can also revoke access from your Slack workspace at **Settings → Integrations → Manage apps**.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Fluso can't see a private channel">
    Add the Fluso bot user to the private channel, or re-run the authorization and select the channel.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Multiple workspaces">
    Connect each workspace separately. Fluso treats them as independent and you can ask "What did I miss in [workspace] #channel?" to disambiguate.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Token expired">
    OAuth tokens can expire. Click **Reconnect** in Settings → Connectors.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
