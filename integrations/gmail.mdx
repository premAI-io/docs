Gmail is the highest-value integration. It's where most work originates, so connecting it gives Fluso the richest context about your day. **Connect this first.**

## What it unlocks

| Capability | Example |
|---|---|
| **Inbox triage** | *"Summarise my unread emails from today."* |
| **Smart search** | *"Find the email where the client sent the pricing spreadsheet."* |
| **Draft replies** | *"Reply to Sarah — I'll have estimates by Thursday."* |
| **Auto-task extraction** | Tasks created automatically from actionable emails. |
| **Thread summaries** | *"Summarise the email thread about the vendor negotiation."* |
| **Follow-up tracking** | *"Has the client responded about the contract?"* |

For the full feature deep dive, see [Email](/features/email).

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings → Connectors">
    Click **Connect** on the Gmail card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize with Google">
    Sign in with the Google account you want Fluso to assist with.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review permissions">
    | Permission | Why |
    |---|---|
    | Read emails | Summarise the inbox, search, extract tasks. |
    | Send emails | Draft and send replies — always with your approval. |
    | View metadata | Headers used to categorise messages. |

    Fluso does **not** request permission to delete email or change account settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Gmail shows as **Connected**. Setup time: about 30 seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Permissions in detail

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Read emails">
    Fluso reads emails when you ask it to (e.g., "summarise my inbox") and when running auto-task extraction. Emails are processed for the response and not stored beyond what's needed for your knowledge graph and task references.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Send emails">
    Fluso never sends an email without your explicit approval. Drafts are always shown for review first.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Metadata">
    Headers are used for categorisation (sender, subject, labels, date) and are not shared with third parties.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Disconnecting

From **Settings → Connectors**, click **Disconnect** on the Gmail card. All Gmail tokens are deleted immediately and Fluso stops accessing your inbox.

You can also revoke access directly from your [Google Account → Security → Third-party apps](https://myaccount.google.com/permissions).

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Fluso isn't finding my emails">
    Verify Gmail is **Connected** in Settings → Connectors. Try disconnecting and reconnecting. If the issue persists, you may have hit a Gmail API rate limit — wait a few minutes and try again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Token expired or app not connected">
    OAuth tokens can expire. Go to Settings → Connectors and click **Reconnect**. Refresh the page once reconnected.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Auto-tasks aren't appearing">
    Auto-task extraction runs periodically. Give it 24–48 hours to learn the patterns of your inbox. You can also trigger it manually with *"Scan my inbox for tasks."*
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
