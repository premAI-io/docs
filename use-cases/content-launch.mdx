Marketing asks: *"We need a landing page for the new product launch, a blog post announcing it, and social media graphics. Can you have it by Friday?"*

It's Wednesday. You have two days. Old plan: panic.

## With Fluso

### Landing page

> *"Build a landing page for our AI analytics product 'DataLens' — hero with headline 'See your data differently', features section (real-time analytics, natural language queries, auto-insights), social proof (3 customer logos), pricing (3 tiers), CTA. Modern, clean design, brand colour #6366F1 primary."*

Fluso creates the full page. You review, tweak the copy, done.

### Blog post

> *"Write a 1,200-word blog post announcing the DataLens launch — cover the problem, how DataLens solves it, key features, and a customer quote. Professional but approachable tone. Include a CTA to sign up for early access."*

Fluso writes the post. You review, add the customer quote, done.

### Social graphics

> *"Generate 5 social media graphics for the DataLens launch — 1080x1080, brand colours, featuring key stats: 'Real-time analytics', '10x faster insights', 'No SQL required'."*

Fluso generates the graphics. You pick the best ones, done.

**Total time: 45 minutes** instead of 2–3 days.

## Variations

### Sequenced campaign

> *"Plan a 5-day launch campaign for DataLens — daily LinkedIn post, X/Twitter thread, and email sequence. Tease day 1, announce day 3, customer story day 5."*

Fluso drafts the full sequence. You schedule.

### Demo deck for sales

> *"Create a 10-slide sales demo deck for DataLens — problem, solution, demo flow, customer proof, pricing, next steps. Modern design, brand colours."*

Pairs with the landing page so messaging is consistent.

### Customer email

> *"Draft a launch email for our existing customer base announcing DataLens — friendly tone, focus on what's new and how to get access. Include a link to the landing page."*

Combines content creation with the email integration.

## Tips for content quality

<Tip>
  **Specify brand and audience.** "For technical buyers — Heads of Data Engineering at companies with 100-1,000 employees" produces sharper output than no audience description.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Iterate.** Drafts are starting points. "Make the headline more concrete" or "Add a Tom Tunguz-style data point in the intro" are valid follow-ups.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Use brand assets you already have.** Tell Fluso "Use our brand colours: #6366F1, #1E1B4B, white" and it stays consistent across all artifacts.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  **Personalise customer-facing content.** Fluso's output is excellent but external content benefits from your voice. Read aloud before publishing — if a sentence sounds like marketing slop, rewrite it.
</Warning>
