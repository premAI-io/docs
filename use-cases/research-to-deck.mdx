A common ask: "Can you put together a competitive analysis of the top 5 AI agent platforms? I need it for the board meeting on Thursday."

It's Monday. You have three days.

## Without Fluso

| Day | Activity | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Google "AI agent platforms," open 20 tabs, read, take notes, get distracted | 3–4 hours |
| Tuesday | Continue reading. Organise findings. Realise you need more on pricing. Search more. | 3–4 hours |
| Wednesday | Build the deck. Write slides. Find images. Format. Polish. | 5–6 hours |

**Total: 12–14 hours over 3 days.**

## With Fluso

### Monday, 9:00 AM

> *"Research the top 5 enterprise AI agent platforms — compare pricing, features, target market, funding, and competitive positioning. Create a 12-slide board presentation with citations."*

### 5–10 minutes later

Fluso delivers two artifacts:

<Card title="Research report">
- Market overview and sizing
- Profiles of 5 platforms with comparison tables
- Pricing analysis
- Funding and growth data
- Competitive advantages and vulnerabilities
- 25+ cited sources
</Card>

<Card title="Presentation">
- 12 professionally designed slides
- Charts comparing pricing and features
- Key takeaways for board discussion
- Speaker notes with context
</Card>

### Monday, 9:15 AM

Review the deck. Tweak 3 slides. Add your personal insights.

### Monday, 10:00 AM

Done. Deck ready. **Three days early.**

**Total time: ~1 hour, including review and customization.**

## Variations

### Lighter weight

> *"Quick research on Rippling's product, pricing, and recent funding. 5-bullet summary, no deck."*

For when you just need to sound informed in your next meeting.

### Source-constrained

> *"Research enterprise AI adoption trends — only sources from 2025 or later, prefer industry analyst reports over blogs."*

Fluso scopes the search and cites accordingly.

### Internal + external

> *"Research our top 3 competitors. Combine public information with what we've discussed about them in emails and Slack."*

Layered intelligence — public data plus your knowledge graph.

## Tips for great research output

<Tip>
  **Be explicit about scope.** "Top 5 platforms compared on pricing, features, target market, funding" beats "research AI platforms." Specificity drives quality.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Ask for the artifact you need.** Saying "create a 12-slide board presentation with citations" gets you the final deliverable. Saying "research it" gets you a report, which you'd then have to convert.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Verify critical claims.** Citations are linked. For high-stakes decisions, click through to the sources before quoting them in front of the board.
</Tip>
