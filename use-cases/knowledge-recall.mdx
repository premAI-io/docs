You're in a meeting with a potential partner. They mention a company you researched three months ago. Your colleague looks at you — do you remember the details?

## Without Fluso

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search email">
    Find 2 threads from January.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search Slack">
    Find a discussion from January.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Try to remember">
    Vague recall.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Give a vague answer">
    *"I think we looked at them… they were interesting but I don't remember the specifics."*
  </Step>
</Steps>

## With Fluso

You discreetly type:

> *"What do I know about Company X?"*

Fluso responds:

> *"You researched Company X on January 15. They're a Series B fintech startup, $30M raised from a16z. Their product competes with Stripe in the APAC market. In your January Slack discussion with the team, the conclusion was: interesting tech but not a fit for partnership due to competitive overlap. You haven't interacted with them since."*

You answer:

> *"Ah yes, Company X — we evaluated them in January. Solid tech but some competitive overlap made a partnership complex. They're worth watching in APAC though."*

You look informed and prepared — because Fluso remembered what you would have forgotten.

## Useful recall queries

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| **Person history** | *"What's the history of our relationship with Acme Corp?"* |
| **Decision recall** | *"What did we decide about the Q3 budget in the April planning meeting?"* |
| **Topic across time** | *"What have we discussed about AI regulation?"* |
| **Project status** | *"Where does the Acme deal stand right now?"* |
| **Who knows what** | *"Who on the team has worked on the authentication system?"* |
| **Connection finder** | *"Is there any link between the Acme deal and the Stripe conversation?"* |
| **Last contact** | *"When did I last talk to the Stripe team?"* |
| **Relationship health** | *"How's the Acme relationship going? Any red flags?"* |

## How recall scales over time

The knowledge graph compounds. The more you use Fluso, the better recall gets:

| Time | Recall quality |
|---|---|
| Week 1 | Frequent contacts, current projects, recent conversations. |
| Month 1 | History of key relationships, project status, communication patterns. |
| Month 3 | Patterns you wouldn't see yourself — neglected relationships, stalled projects, recurring topics. |
| Month 6+ | Institutional memory. *"What was the outcome of that vendor negotiation in January?"* — answered instantly. |

## When recall is most valuable

- **Mid-meeting**: discreetly query while someone is talking.
- **Pre-call**: ask before joining ("Brief me on Sarah from Acme").
- **Weekly review**: ask "What slipped this week?" or "What's stalled?"
- **Quarterly planning**: ask "What were our Q1 commitments and which did we hit?"

## Privacy

Your knowledge graph is yours alone. Stored in your isolated workspace, never shared with other users, never used to train models. You can delete individual entries or the entire graph at any time.

For more, see [Security & privacy](/resources/security).
