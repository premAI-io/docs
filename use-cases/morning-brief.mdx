The most common workflow on day one. Connect Gmail and Calendar, then run this every morning.

## The old way

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Gmail">
    47 unread emails. Scan through. Read the ones that look important.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open three replies">
    Realise you need to check Slack before replying to one. Switch tabs. Get distracted by 5 new messages. Switch back. Forget what you were doing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add tasks manually">
    Find your task manager. Type in 4 tasks. Set deadlines.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check calendar">
    Realise you have a meeting in 15 minutes you haven't prepared for. Scramble through emails to find context.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Total time: 45–75 minutes. Every day.**

## With Fluso

> *"Morning brief"*

<Card title="What Fluso delivers" icon="sun">
**📧 Email** — 47 unread. 4 need action, 8 are FYI, 35 are noise.
- Task created: "Review contract from Legal" (due Friday)
- Task created: "Send Q2 numbers to CFO" (due tomorrow)
- Draft ready: reply to client about timeline
- Draft ready: reply to Sarah about proposal changes

**📅 Today** — 3 meetings.
- 10am standup (15 min)
- 2pm client call with Acme (prep ready)
- 4pm 1:1 with Lisa

**✅ Tasks due today** — 2 (Q2 report, PR review).

**🔔 Follow-ups needed** — 1 (Legal hasn't responded to Monday's question — draft nudge ready).
</Card>

**Total time: 2 minutes.** Open Fluso, say "morning brief," review, done.

## Variations

| Prompt | What it does |
|---|---|
| *"Morning brief"* | The standard — inbox + calendar + tasks + follow-ups. |
| *"Just my unread emails"* | Skip the rest, focus on inbox. |
| *"What's urgent today?"* | Only items that need attention now. |
| *"What did I miss while I was out?"* | Multi-day catch-up after vacation or focus time. |

## Setup checklist

- [ ] Gmail connected — see [Gmail](/integrations/gmail).
- [ ] Calendar connected — see [Google Calendar](/integrations/google-calendar).
- [ ] Auto-task extraction enabled (default).
- [ ] (Optional) Slack connected — pulls team context into the brief.

## What "good" looks like after a week

After running this for five days:

- You stop opening Gmail in the morning. The brief is enough.
- You batch-approve drafts at the start of the day instead of writing them.
- Your task list builds itself from email and meetings.
- You walk into your first meeting prepared, not scrambling.

<Tip>
  **Run it at the same time every day.** Consistent timing trains both you and Fluso. The graph gets stronger with regular use.
</Tip>
