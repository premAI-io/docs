Email is the workflow Fluso pays back fastest. The example below is the standard "Monday after a weekend off" version, but the same prompt works on any pile of unread mail.

## What's actually in your inbox

The mix is the same for everyone. Direct requests from your boss and team. Client emails about projects in flight. Vendor mail about contracts and pricing. Newsletters you subscribed to in 2022 and never unsubscribed from. Notification digests from every SaaS tool you use. Calendar invites and updates. Automated reports.

You spend half an hour to an hour every morning sorting through it. Some days an important message gets buried and you only realise when someone follows up.

## What changes

Monday morning. You haven't checked email since Friday afternoon. Ninety-four unread.

> *"Summarise my unread emails since Friday."*

Fluso reads all 94 and replies with three buckets.

**Needs action — 6**

1. Legal: contract signed, needs final review by Tuesday. Task created, due Tuesday.
2. Sarah at Acme: can we move the launch date? Wants to discuss tomorrow. Draft reply ready: *"Available tomorrow 2-4pm, which works best?"*
3. CFO: needs Q2 revenue breakdown by Wednesday's board meeting.
4. Engineering: production hotfix ready for your approval.
5. HR: new hire starts Monday, please review onboarding plan.
6. Client: API issues, can someone look at it?

**FYI — 12**

Weekly metrics (revenue +3%, users +5%). Product team roadmap update. Three vendor announcements. Two project status updates. Four newsletter digests that might actually be worth reading.

**Noise — 76**

Notification digests, automated alerts, marketing.

You spend five minutes. You read the six action items. You approve the draft reply to Sarah. You bump one task priority. You glance at the FYI bucket and skip everything else. The 94 emails are handled, and the four things that mattered are tracked.

Forty-five minutes saved. Nothing important missed.

## Useful follow-ups

After the initial sweep, you can sharpen:

> *"Show me only emails from Acme this week."*

> *"Anything from my boss I haven't responded to?"*

> *"Find emails with attachments from the last 24 hours."*

> *"Did the client respond about the contract yet?"*

## Drafting replies in batch

Once you've seen the action list, batch the writing instead of one-at-a-time:

> *"Draft replies to the three client emails. Keep each under four sentences."*

> *"Reply to Legal. Confirm I'll have feedback by EOD Tuesday."*

> *"Reply to the CFO. Q2 numbers ready by tomorrow afternoon."*

You read each draft, approve, send. Five minutes total for what would have been forty-five of typing.

<Warning>
  Read external-facing drafts carefully before approving. Fluso has good instincts; you have context Fluso doesn't.
</Warning>

## Setup notes

Gmail connected (see [Gmail](/integrations/gmail)). Auto-task extraction enabled by default. Run for at least three days before judging extraction quality — it tightens fast.
