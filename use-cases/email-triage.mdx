Email is the highest-leverage workflow Fluso handles. This page walks through what triage looks like end to end.

## The old way

Your inbox is a mix of:

- Direct requests from your boss and team.
- Client emails about projects.
- Vendor emails about contracts and pricing.
- Newsletters you subscribed to months ago.
- Notification digests from every SaaS tool you use.
- Calendar invites and updates.
- Automated reports and alerts.

You spend 30–60 minutes every morning sorting through this. Some days an important email gets buried and you miss it until someone follows up.

## With Fluso

It's Monday morning. You haven't checked email since Friday afternoon.

> *"Summarise my unread emails since Friday."*

Fluso reads 94 unread emails and returns:

<Card title="Needs action (6)">
1. 🚨 Legal — "Contract signed, need final review by Tuesday." → Task created, due Tuesday.
2. Sarah (Acme) — "Can we move the launch date? Let's discuss tomorrow." → Draft reply: "Available tomorrow 2-4pm, which works best?"
3. CFO — "Need Q2 revenue breakdown by Wednesday board meeting."
4. Engineering — "Production hotfix ready for your approval."
5. HR — "New hire starts Monday — please review onboarding plan."
6. Client — "Having issues with the API — can someone look at this?"
</Card>

<Card title="FYI (12)">
- Weekly metrics (revenue +3%, users +5%)
- Product team roadmap update
- 3 vendor announcements
- 2 project status updates
- 4 newsletter digests
</Card>

<Card title="Noise (76)">
Notification digests, automated alerts, marketing emails.
</Card>

**You spend 5 minutes.** Review the 6 action items, approve the draft reply to Sarah, adjust one task priority, done.

**Time saved: 45 minutes.** And nothing important slipped.

## Useful follow-ups

After the initial triage, sharpen the focus:

> *"Show me only emails from Acme this week."*

> *"Anything from my boss I haven't responded to?"*

> *"Find emails with attachments from the last 24 hours."*

> *"Did the client respond about the contract yet?"*

## When to draft new replies

Once you see the action list, batch the replies:

> *"Draft replies to the three client emails — keep each under 4 sentences."*

> *"Reply to Legal — confirm I'll have feedback by EOD Tuesday."*

> *"Reply to the CFO — say I'll have Q2 numbers ready by tomorrow afternoon."*

You review and approve. Fluso sends.

<Warning>
  Always review drafts before approving — especially for sensitive or external-facing email. Fluso is good but you have context it doesn't.
</Warning>
